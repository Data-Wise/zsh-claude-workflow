# Draft Document

General-purpose drafting for various document types.

## Required Input

- **Document Type**: Email, memo, report, proposal, etc.
- **Purpose**: What should this accomplish?
- **Audience**: Who will read this?
- **Tone**: Formal, informal, technical, persuasive
- **Key Points**: Main messages to convey

## Process

1. **Clarify Purpose**: What action or understanding is needed?
2. **Know Audience**: Technical level, relationship, expectations
3. **Outline Structure**: Logical flow of information
4. **Draft Content**: Clear, concise language
5. **Review & Polish**: Check tone, clarity, completeness

## Document Templates

### Email (Professional)

```markdown
Subject: [Clear, specific subject line]

[Greeting],

[Opening - context/purpose in 1-2 sentences]

[Body - main content, organized in short paragraphs or bullets]

[Action requested or next steps]

[Professional closing],
[Name]
```

### Memo

```markdown
**TO**: [Recipients]
**FROM**: [Sender]
**DATE**: [Date]
**RE**: [Subject]

---

**Summary**: [1-2 sentence overview]

**Background**: [Context for the issue]

**Analysis/Discussion**: [Main content]

**Recommendation**: [What you're proposing]

**Next Steps**: [Actions needed]
```

### Brief Report

```markdown
# [Report Title]

## Executive Summary
[Key findings and recommendations in 3-5 sentences]

## Background
[Context and purpose]

## Findings
### [Finding 1]
[Details]

### [Finding 2]
[Details]

## Recommendations
1. [Recommendation 1]
2. [Recommendation 2]

## Appendix (if needed)
[Supporting details]
```

### Proposal

```markdown
# [Proposal Title]

## Problem/Opportunity
[What needs to be addressed]

## Proposed Solution
[What you're proposing]

## Benefits
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]

## Implementation
[How this would work]

## Resources Required
[Time, money, people]

## Timeline
[Key milestones]

## Conclusion
[Summary and call to action]
```

## Tone Adjustments

### Formal
- Full sentences
- Professional vocabulary
- Third person often
- "Please find attached..."

### Informal
- Contractions okay
- First/second person
- Conversational
- "Here's what we found..."

### Technical
- Precise terminology
- Detailed explanations
- Assume domain knowledge
- Include specifications

### Persuasive
- Benefit-focused
- Address objections
- Call to action
- Evidence-based arguments

## Example Prompts

"Draft an email to my department chair requesting funding for a conference. Formal tone. I'm presenting a paper at JSM."

"Write a memo summarizing our simulation study results for the research team. Include recommendations for next steps."

"Create a brief proposal for developing a new course on causal inference. Include course objectives, target audience, and resource needs."

"Draft a response to a student asking for a deadline extension. Be understanding but maintain standards."

## Tips

1. **Lead with Purpose**: Don't bury the main point
2. **One Idea per Paragraph**: Easy to scan and understand
3. **Active Voice**: More direct and engaging
4. **Be Specific**: Vague requests get vague responses
5. **Proofread**: Read aloud to catch errors
6. **Consider Timing**: When will this be read?
