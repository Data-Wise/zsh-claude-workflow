# Write Response

Draft professional responses for various contexts.

## Purpose

Create well-crafted responses for:
- Email replies
- Reviewer comments (non-academic)
- Colleague requests
- Support inquiries
- General correspondence

## Required Input

- **Context**: What you're responding to
- **Relationship**: Professional, formal, casual
- **Tone**: Helpful, firm, apologetic, etc.
- **Key points**: What must be addressed

## Process

1. **Understand the request**
   - What are they asking/saying?
   - What's the underlying need?
   - What's the relationship context?

2. **Plan the response**
   - Key points to address
   - Appropriate tone
   - Desired outcome

3. **Draft response**
   - Clear and concise
   - Professional tone
   - Actionable next steps

4. **Review**
   - Check tone
   - Verify completeness
   - Remove unnecessary content

## Output Format

```markdown
## Response Draft

**Context**: [Brief summary of what you're responding to]

**Tone**: [Professional/Friendly/Formal/etc.]

---

[Greeting]

[Opening - acknowledge their message]

[Body - address main points]

[Next steps or call to action]

[Closing]

[Sign-off]

---

**Notes**: [Any considerations or alternatives]
```

## Response Templates

### Acknowledging a Request
```
Thank you for reaching out about [topic].

I've reviewed [item/request] and [response].

[Next steps or timeline]

Please let me know if you have any questions.
```

### Declining Politely
```
Thank you for thinking of me for [opportunity].

After careful consideration, I'm unable to [commitment] at this time due to [brief reason].

I appreciate your understanding and wish you [well wishes].
```

### Following Up
```
I wanted to follow up on [previous topic].

[Status update or question]

Please let me know [specific request].
```

### Providing Information
```
Thank you for your question about [topic].

[Answer/Information]

For more details, you can [resource/next step].
```

## Tone Adjustments

### Formal
- Full sentences
- Professional greetings
- No contractions
- Structured format

### Friendly Professional
- Warmer language
- Some contractions okay
- Personalized touches
- Conversational flow

### Direct
- Brief sentences
- Clear action items
- Minimal pleasantries
- Bottom-line upfront

## Tips

- Read the original message twice
- Address all questions asked
- Be clear about next steps
- Match formality to relationship
- Keep it concise
- Proofread before sending
