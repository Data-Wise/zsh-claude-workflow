# Write Cover Letter

Draft cover letters for journal submissions or professional correspondence.

## Required Input

- **Type**: Journal submission, job application, grant, or other
- **Recipient**: Editor name, journal, or organization
- **Document**: Paper title, position, or proposal being submitted
- **Key Points**: What to emphasize (optional)

## Process

1. **Identify Purpose**: What action do you want?
2. **Highlight Fit**: Why this venue/position?
3. **Summarize Contribution**: Brief overview
4. **Address Requirements**: Conflicts, permissions, etc.
5. **Professional Close**: Clear call to action

## Output Format

### Journal Submission Cover Letter

```markdown
[Date]

[Editor Name], Editor-in-Chief
[Journal Name]

Dear [Dr./Prof. Name or "Editors"],

We are pleased to submit our manuscript titled "[Title]" for consideration for publication in [Journal Name].

**Summary**: [2-3 sentences describing the paper and its main contribution]

**Significance**: [1-2 sentences on why this matters and fits the journal's scope]

**Highlights**:
- [Key finding/contribution 1]
- [Key finding/contribution 2]
- [Key finding/contribution 3]

[Optional: Special considerations - methods paper, invited resubmission, etc.]

We confirm that:
- This manuscript is original and not under consideration elsewhere
- All authors have approved the submission
- [Any required statements: conflicts of interest, data availability, etc.]

We suggest the following potential reviewers:
1. [Name], [Institution] - [Brief reason]
2. [Name], [Institution] - [Brief reason]

[Optional: Reviewers to exclude with brief justification]

Thank you for considering our work. We look forward to hearing from you.

Sincerely,

[Corresponding Author Name]
[Title, Institution]
[Email]
[ORCID if applicable]

On behalf of all authors
```

### Revision Cover Letter

```markdown
Dear [Editor Name],

Thank you for the opportunity to revise our manuscript "[Title]" (Manuscript ID: [ID]).

We have carefully addressed all reviewer comments and made substantial revisions. Key changes include:

1. [Major change 1]
2. [Major change 2]
3. [Major change 3]

A detailed point-by-point response to all reviewer comments is included as a separate document.

We believe these revisions have substantially strengthened the manuscript and hope you find it suitable for publication in [Journal Name].

Sincerely,
[Author]
```

## Cover Letter Types

### Journal Submission
- Summarize contribution
- Explain fit with journal scope
- Suggest reviewers
- Required declarations

### Job/Position Application
- Express interest and fit
- Highlight relevant qualifications
- Reference specific job requirements
- Request interview/next steps

### Grant Cover Letter
- State funding request
- Summarize project significance
- Note alignment with funder priorities
- Brief qualifications statement

## Journal-Specific Elements

### Statistical Journals
- Emphasize methodological novelty
- Note theoretical contributions
- Mention software/reproducibility

### Applied Journals
- Emphasize practical implications
- Note relevance to field
- Highlight real data application

### Medical/Health Journals
- Confirm ethical approval
- State clinical trial registration
- Declare conflicts of interest
- ICMJE compliance statement

## Example Prompts

"Write a cover letter for submitting our mediation methods paper to Psychological Methods. The paper introduces a new test for sequential mediation. Suggest relevant reviewers."

"Draft a revision cover letter for JASA. We've addressed all comments and added new simulations as requested by Reviewer 2."

"Create a cover letter for a postdoc application at [University] in quantitative psychology. Emphasize methods development experience."

## Tips

1. **Be Concise**: Editors are busy - one page maximum
2. **Know the Journal**: Reference recent relevant papers if appropriate
3. **Be Specific**: Generic letters are obvious
4. **Proofread**: Errors suggest carelessness
5. **Follow Guidelines**: Some journals have specific requirements
6. **Suggest Reviewers**: Make editor's job easier (but be ethical)
